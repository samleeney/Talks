Standard searches sacrifice information by compressing data and separating interference mitigation. We introduce a framework operating directly on full time–frequency dynamic spectra, using the Bayes factor for rigorous detection. This enables targeted, hypothesis-driven searches tailored to specific astrophysical signatures. Crucially, we employ joint inference: simultaneously searching for pulses and flagging RFI. Novel analytic marginalisations—integrating over amplitude, noise, and arrival time—ensure computational feasibility. Because the algorithm is inherently parallelisable, scaling with available GPU memory, this rigorous Bayesian approach is now practical for real-time, survey-scale data.
